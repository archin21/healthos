# HealthOS — Complete Replit Build Specification
## Hackathon Prototype | UI Shell + Minimal Backend | B2C HealthTech

---

## 0. PRODUCT OVERVIEW

**Product Name:** HealthOS  
**Tagline:** "Your health, finally decoded."  
**Core Concept:** A unified health intelligence platform that standardises lab reports across Indian diagnostic labs, tracks health trends over time, screens for mental health, and helps users find affordable generic medicine alternatives.  
**Target User:** Indian urban professional, 25–50, who gets annual health checks but can't meaningfully interpret or compare their reports.  
**Business Model:** B2C Subscription (Freemium — 1 report free, subscription for trends + mental health + medicine tab)  
**Build Mode:** UI Shell first. Minimal backend. Prioritise visual polish over working functionality. This is a hackathon demo for internal ISB judges.

---

**Assessment Cards (4 cards, 2x2 grid on desktop):**

Each card shows:
- Test name and full form
- What it screens for
- Number of questions
- Time to complete
- Last completed date (or "Not yet taken")
- Status badge: Not Started / Completed / Retake
- "Start Assessment" button

Card 1 — PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire)
- Screens for: Depression severity
- 9 questions | ~3 minutes
- Scoring: 0–4 None, 5–9 Mild, 10–14 Moderate, 15–19 Moderately Severe, 20–27 Severe

Card 2 — GAD-7 (Generalised Anxiety Disorder Scale)
- Screens for: Anxiety severity
- 7 questions | ~2 minutes
- Scoring: 0–4 Minimal, 5–9 Mild, 10–14 Moderate, 15–21 Severe

Card 3 — PSS (Perceived Stress Scale)
- Screens for: Perceived stress levels
- 10 questions | ~3 minutes
- Scoring: 0–13 Low, 14–26 Moderate, 27–40 High

Card 4 — DASS-21 (Depression Anxiety Stress Scale)
- Screens for: Depression, anxiety and stress simultaneously
- 21 questions | ~5 minutes
- Scoring: Separate subscale scores for each dimension

**Assessment Flow (modal, triggered by "Start Assessment"):**
- Progress bar at top: "Question 3 of 9"
- Question text: Large, readable
- Response options: Likert scale buttons (Not at all / Several days / More than half the days / Nearly every day)
- Previous / Next navigation
- On completion: show results immediately

**Results View (after completing PHQ-9 as example):**
- Score: "12 / 27"
- Band: "Moderate Depression"
- Colour: Amber
- Plain English: "Your responses suggest you may be experiencing moderate levels of depressive symptoms. This doesn't mean you have depression — but it's worth paying attention to."
- What this means: 3 bullet points in plain language
- Recommendations:
  - "Consider speaking with a counsellor or therapist"
  - "iCall (free, India-based): 9152987821"
  - "Vandrevala Foundation Helpline: 1860-2662-345 (24x7)"
  - "Nimhans Connect: nimhansconnect.in"
- Retake reminder: "We recommend retaking this assessment every 4 weeks to track changes"
- "Download Results" button (PDF shell — no actual generation needed for prototype)

**Mental Health Score Integration:**
- Mental health scores appear SEPARATELY from the physical Health Score (0–100)
- A second score panel: "Mental Wellness Score: 64/100"
- Calculated as: weighted average of inverted PHQ-9, GAD-7, PSS, DASS-21 subscales, normalised to 0–100
- Shown on Dashboard as a separate card alongside physical Health Score
- Tooltip: "This score reflects your self-reported mental wellness across 4 validated screening tools. It is not a clinical diagnosis."
- Nuance note in UI: "Physical and mental health scores are shown separately — combined scores can obscure important signals in either dimension."

## 9. ONE-PAGER BRIEF (HealthOS)

**To be produced as a separate clean PDF document**

**Title:** HealthOS — Your Health, Finally Decoded

**Problem:**
India generates 500M+ diagnostic reports annually. Most people receive a 12-page PDF with no context, can't compare results across labs (SRL vs Thyrocare vs Medanta use different names, units, and ranges for the same test), and have no way to track their health trajectory over time. Mental health goes entirely unscreened. And 80% of patients overpay for branded drugs when generics with identical salts are available for 80% less.

**Why AI:**
Indian lab reports have wildly inconsistent formatting — rules-based parsing breaks. LLMs handle layout variation, extract structured data from any format, and generate plain-English explanations personalised to the patient's age and gender. AI makes the 500M reports that nobody reads into health intelligence that actually changes behaviour.

**Product Concept:**
HealthOS is a B2C health intelligence platform with four modules:
1. Smart Report — AI-parsed, standardised lab reports with plain-English explanations and a 0-100 Health Score
2. Health Trends — unified tracking of parameters across labs and time
3. Mental Wellness — PHQ-9, GAD-7, PSS, DASS-21 screening with scoring, interpretation, and referral nudges
4. Medicine Finder — generic alternatives to branded drugs, matched at salt level, CDSCO-approved only

**Customer:**
Indian urban professional, 25–50, who gets an annual health check but doesn't act on it. The trigger: receiving a lab report they can't interpret. The job: "Tell me what's wrong, what to watch, and what to do about it."

**Business Case:**
- TAM: 200M+ urban Indians with health insurance or disposable income for health spend
- Revenue: ₹299/month subscription (unlimited reports, all modules)
- CAC: Low — organic via lab report sharing (report-as-referral loop)
- Moat: Standardisation dataset (260+ params mapped across 6 lab chains, validated by doctors across 1,000+ reports) takes years to build
- Comparable: Zus Health (US, $40M Series B) — HealthOS is the India-first version

**Ask (for hackathon context):**
Validation and feedback. What works? What would make you pay ₹299/month?
